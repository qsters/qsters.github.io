SpaceX Launched Starship Rocket on Fourth Test Flight

Date: June 6, 2024

SpaceX successfully launched its Starship rocket on the fourth test flight, achieving a significant milestone in its quest to create a fully reusable spacecraft. The launch took place from the company's Boca Chica launch site in Texas.

This test flight evaluated the performance of Starship's engines, aerodynamic surfaces, and overall structural integrity during ascent and descent. The ultimate goal of this mission was to successfully land the Starship prototype back on the ground, paving the way for future missions to Mars and beyond.

Key Objectives Achieved:

  • Tested the enhanced Raptor engines.
  • Assessed the aerodynamics and thermal protection system during re-entry.
  • Demonstrated controlled descent and landing capabilities.

Space enthusiasts and experts alike celebrated this launch, which represented a crucial step in SpaceX's ambitious plans for interplanetary travel. This mission continues to push the boundaries of space exploration.

Earth’s Aphelion

Date: July 5, 2024

At 05:06 UTC, the Earth will reach aphelion—the point in its orbit farthest from the Sun. This is an excellent time to observe the distance between the Earth and the Sun.

Buck Moon

Date: July 21, 2024

July’s Full Moon, known as the Buck Moon, will signify the new antlers that emerge on deer buck’s foreheads around this time. This year, it falls at 10:17 UTC.

Perseid Meteors

Date: August 12-13, 2024

The Perseid meteor shower is usually one of the most active and brightest meteor showers of the year. Despite a bright First Quarter Moon, the Perseids will still offer a spectacular show.

Conjunction of Mars and Jupiter

Date: August 14, 2024

Mars and Jupiter will pass within 0.31 degrees of each other in the pre-dawn sky. This close approach is one of the notable conjunctions before the year 2040.

Blue Sturgeon Moon

Date: August 19, 2024

August’s Full Moon, traditionally called the Sturgeon Moon, will also be a seasonal Blue Moon—the third Full Moon in a season with four Full Moons. This rare event is not to be missed.
